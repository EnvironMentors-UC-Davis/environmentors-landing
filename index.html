The program

Mentor a future scientist. Strengthen the science you do today.

Every year, UC Davis grad students, postdocs, and faculty pair up with high school students from the Sacramento region. Mentees design and run their own environmental research projects, present at the UC Davis Science Fair, and a top cohort travels to the GCSE International Science Fair in Panama.

For mentors, it's a chance to translate your research into something a teenager can do, see, and own. For your science, it's the broader impact you're already supposed to be building — done in a way that's real instead of performative.

"Thanks to the program and my mentor, I feel prepared with my speaking skills and am confident in my research and writing skills." — Erin Murphy, 2015 Emerging Environmental Leader Award winner

For faculty

A turnkey way to deliver on the broader-impact requirements that come with NSF, USDA, and most federal grants — without inventing a program from scratch. We handle the mentee side; your lab provides the science.

For grad students & postdocs

Year-long mentorship that translates directly into job applications, grant biosketches, and the kind of teaching credential search committees actually care about. Bi-weekly meetings, with summer free.
